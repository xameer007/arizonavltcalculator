What is Arizona Vehicle License Tax (VLT)?

The Arizona Vehicle License Tax (VLT) is an annual tax levied on vehicles registered in the state of Arizona. Unlike traditional sales tax that's paid once at purchase, the VLT is recalculated each year based on your vehicle's depreciated value, making it a recurring cost of vehicle ownership in Arizona.

Arizona's VLT system is unique among U.S. states. Instead of charging a standard vehicle registration fee, Arizona uses a percentage-based system where you pay a tax based on 60% of your vehicle's manufacturer's base retail price, which depreciates over time. This means newer, more expensive vehicles pay higher VLT, while older vehicles pay progressively less each year.

üí° Key Point: The VLT rate in Arizona is currently 2.80% for most passenger vehicles. However, this percentage is applied to the assessed value (60% of MSRP) rather than the full vehicle value, and the assessed value decreases by 16.25% each year.

How Arizona VLT Differs from Other States

Most states charge a flat registration fee regardless of vehicle value, typically ranging from $50 to $200 annually. Arizona's percentage-based VLT system can result in significantly higher costs, especially for new and luxury vehicles. However, the depreciation schedule means the tax burden decreases substantially over time.

Frequently Asked Questions About Arizona VLT

Q: Is Arizona VLT the same as sales tax?
No, VLT is not the same as sales tax. While many states charge sales tax once when you purchase a vehicle, Arizona charges VLT annually instead. The VLT is a recurring annual tax based on your vehicle's depreciated assessed value at a rate of 2.80%.
Q: Can I deduct Arizona VLT on my federal income taxes?
Yes, Arizona VLT may be deductible on your federal income tax return. Since VLT is a tax based on value rather than a flat fee, it typically qualifies as a deductible personal property tax if you itemize deductions. Consult with a tax professional for guidance.
Q: What happens if I move out of Arizona mid-year?
If you move out of Arizona and register your vehicle in another state, you generally cannot get a refund for the VLT you've already paid. VLT covers the full registration year regardless of when you move.
Q: Do I pay VLT on a leased vehicle?
Yes, lessees must pay VLT on leased vehicles. The VLT is calculated the same way as for owned vehicles, based on the vehicle's assessed value. Often, the leasing company includes the VLT amount in your monthly lease payment.
Q: How does VLT work for electric vehicles (EVs)?
Electric vehicles pay VLT using the same calculation as gas-powered vehicles - 2.80% of the assessed value. However, EVs also pay an additional annual fee ($147 as of recent years) to compensate for not paying gas taxes that fund road maintenance.
Q: Does VLT apply to motorcycles and RVs?
Yes, motorcycles, RVs, trailers, and other registered vehicles are subject to Arizona VLT using the same 2.80% rate and depreciation schedule. The assessed value is based on 60% of the manufacturer's base retail price.
